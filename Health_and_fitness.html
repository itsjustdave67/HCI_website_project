Health and Fitness

  • On health matters, it's very important for you as a mother to be cautious for both you and your baby. This in context of what you consume, of which defitnitely affects your baby. Your Physical health and Mental health provide a heavy contribution in a successful childbirth.
  • That is why we provide these guidelines that will help you achieve a better lifestyle for you and your baby. It directs you on the proper nutrition for each semester and the foods and substances to be wary of.
  • You might tell yourself,” Oh, well since I’m expectant I’m not supposed to engage in any exercise or I’m I supposed to do any exercises while I’m expectant?” Yes, you actually do. Matter of fact some health professionals recommend it as a necessity for an easier labor period and adjustment mechanism.

Health Guidelines

1st Trimester

This is the most crucial time since your baby develops the body structure and organs. This is the stage where you may experience nausea, fatigue, frequent urination, and also breast tenderness don’t panic.

Here is the list of nutrients that you will need at this time:
  • 1. Folic acid- Sources include beans, citrus fruits, and green leafy vegetables.
  • 2. Calcium- Sources include dairy products and also dark leafy vegetables.
  • 3. Iron- Sources include meat, poultry, beans, and also greens.
  • 4. Vitamin B12- Sources include meat, poultry, seafood, and cereals.
  • 5. Omega-3 Fatty Oil- Sources include fatty fish, chia seeds, and flax seeds.

2nd Trimester

This is what many refer to as the “Golden period” as many of the first effects of pregnancy disappear. You get to have decreased nausea better sleep patterns and also there is a general increase in energy. Though you might still succumb to back pain, leg cramps, and also heartburn.

The list of nutrients you'll need are :
  • 1. Iron- Sources include beans, citrus fruits, and green leafy vegetables.
  • 2. Calcium-
  • 3. Magnesium- whose sources are-: nuts and seeds, bananas, and also yogurt
  • 4. Vitamin D- The sun(Nature's best source), milk cereals soy milk, and also egg yolks
  • 5. Omega-3 Fatty Oil-
  • 6. Folic acid-

3rd Trimester

Congratulations you are approaching your final months of pregnancy and soon to be a mother. At this time you might have been experiencing shortness of breath, sleeping problems, varicose veins, and also hemorrhoids. However, this is to tell you that your baby is almost near.

This is what you need :
  • 1. Iron
  • 2. Omega-3 Fatty Oil
  • 3. Folic Acid
  • 4. Magnesium
  • 5. Vitamin D
  • 6. Vitamin B12

The "donts" Of Health and Nutrition

Just Don’t do it. The following can have very serious harmful effects on your baby’s development:
  • Caffeinated drinks

    Caffeinated drinks are for example the variety of espressos and energy drinks. These drinks contains a relatively high amount of sugar that is usually not good for the random human being. Now. In your case as a pregnant it will increase your blood pressure and hand in hand your heart rate. It also causes you to produce more urine than what is expected of a pregnant lady.

    The high caffeine takes a longer period to be removed from your body and therefore has side effects on you. It can make you to become light headed and nauseous. Since all your nutrients consumption are for you and your baby, in the long run the baby also consumes the caffeine. Some studies support the thesis that is causes miscarriages and preterm birth so generally avoid it. Therefore avoid it in all circumstances but if you must drink coffee take only 200mg a day.

  • Alcohol

    Alcohol is an obvious “don’t” during pregnancy. Even you as a person can’t handle liquor well so why should a new being introduced to a new environment be able to cope with it. Your baby in general cannot process alcohol well and their exposure to it is very detrimental to their development.

    Alcohol increases the risks of miscarriages, premature birth, you baby being born below weight, deformation of organs and limbs and later on can cause issues to them after they’re born. In fact it can cause one to develop a serious condition known as Foetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder (FASD).

  • 3. Smoking Cigarettes

    Smoking while pregnant is very risky since in general smoking cigarettes has been deemed as a complete detrimental habit with absolutely no benefits. It causes tissue damage to the brain and lungs of the baby. It also increases the risks of miscarriages and low development of babies due to lack of sufficient oxygen.

  • Under-cooked meat

    All under-cooked meat is bad for both you and the baby since it can cause food poisoning for both you and the baby. The baby’s immune system is still developing and such bacteria exposure can cause still-births. So make sure all your meat whether pregnant or not, is cooked properly.

  • 5. Seafood that contains mercury

    Mercury in any types of food is very poisonous to all human beings. So you can imagine what kind of unfathomable damage it can do to your child. If you have to partake Seafood make sure it is well done.

Fitness Guidelines

Benefits


Some of the general benefits of exercising during pregnancy include:
  • 1. Improved general fitness
  • 2. Reduced back and pelvic pain
  • 3. Improved posture
  • 4. Stress relief
  • 5. Improved blood circulation
  • 6. Fewer delivery complications
  • 7. Weight control
  • 8. Improved sleep and management of insomnia

Exercises


Some of the exercises that you can include in your daily routine include:
  • 1. Low impact cardio - can be achieved by stationary biking and also light jogging.
  • 2. Walking - should be done at a pace that you are comfortable with especially during the 2nd and 3rd trimesters. This is done in short phases in order to prevent fatigue.
  • 3. Squats - they are very effective in improving posture
  • 4. Yoga - can be done as a way of stress relief and meditation
  • 5. Pelvic floor exercises - depending on the trimester your doctor can advice on the ones to do.
  • 6. Kegel exercises These exercises performed to strengthen the pelvic floor muscles, involving repetitions of both sustained and rapid voluntary contractions of the muscles.

The "donts" Of Exercising

Although exercise is good for you there are some that you will need need to avoid when you are expectant. Below are some of the exercises that you need to avoid:
  • Contact Sports

    Contact sports include football, netball, and also volleyball just to name a few. This can pose a danger of being hit.

  • Risky Exercises

    Exercises where falling is likely to happen, this means no bungee jumping, high diving, skiing, and sky diving.

  • 3. Highly Intensive Workouts

    Exercises that use an extensive amount of energy such as jumping, hopping, and running. You can easily get fatigued and have shortness of breath.

Get Informed.

No need to cap on it there are different exercises that are very beneficial for you and your baby's health .So I do say that it is safe to exercise during pregnancy. It’s better to choose a healthier option.

Exercises that are routinely flexible. These can be included on your daily routine since they are easier and much safer to do: Walking, Prenatal yoga, Swimming and Stationary cycling. Just carry a bottle of water around with you as you perform these exercises.

This means that you are doing too much exercise and need to cut down on the amount of strain and effort you put on your body right now. Hydrate and take good rests between your workout sessions. Consult your doctor if the conditions persist with reduced exercise.

The following are some of the few circumstance or exercise to perform:

  • Any high impact exercise.
  • Exercising in hot weather.
  • Exercises that require lying on the back for long periods.

Well, you should exercise since it helps in the following:

  • Improve general fitness
  • Good for your mental health
  • Reduce back pains
  • Promotes healthy weight gain during expectancy
